1. Civil Engineer

Job Description

Civil engineers primarily plan, design, construct, operate and maintain physical infrastructure, including water and waste management facilities, transportation and communications infrastructure and structures and public buildings. This infrastructure supports people’s basic needs, while enabling and driving economic development.

Minimum Qualification Required

Bachelor's Degree or Advanced Diploma (NQF Level 7)

Salary Range

R27 889 to R72 798 per month - 2025

2. Registered Nurse

Job Description

Registered nurses (RNs) are licensed healthcare professionals who work with doctors and medical teams to provide patient-centered care. They often specialize in areas like emergency medicine, pediatrics, cardiology, mental health, or oncology, depending on their interests and training. RNs support patients during hospital stays or routine visits and are key communicators—explaining diagnoses, treatments, and home care instructions in clear terms. Through rigorous education and licensure, RNs are equipped to deliver safe, effective, and compassionate care across a wide range of healthcare settings.

Minimum Qualification Required

Ranges between Bachelor's Degree or Advanced Diploma (NQF Level 7) And Bachelor Honours Degree, Postgraduate Diploma, or Bachelor's Degree (NQF Level 8) based on nursing field

Salary Range

R3 862 to R33 083 per month - 2025

3. Data Scientist

Job Description

A data scientist analyzes large datasets to uncover meaningful insights that help organizations make informed decisions and solve complex problems. Combining expertise in technology, mathematics, business, and communication, they lead research efforts to turn raw data into valuable strategies. By revealing patterns and trends hidden in big data, data scientists play a crucial role in improving operations and driving success—making them highly sought-after professionals, often seen as "rock stars" in today’s data-driven business world.

Minimum Qualification Required

Bachelor's Degree or Advanced Diploma (NQF Level 7)

Salary Range

R20 500 to R65 583 per month - 2025

4. Cybersecurity Analysts

Job Description

Cybersecurity analysts are responsible for safeguarding an organization’s computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats and unauthorized access. They work proactively to identify potential vulnerabilities, defend against attacks, and respond to security breaches when they occur. By protecting valuable digital assets—including hardware, software, and sensitive information—they play a critical role in maintaining the security and integrity of a company’s technology infrastructure.

Minimum Qualification Required

Diploma or Advanced Certificate (NQF Level 6)

Salary Range

R32 076 to R54 421 per month - 2025

5. Agricultural Scientist

Job Description

Agricultural scientists, also known as agriculturists, research farming methods with the goal of boosting productivity. They seek to enhance the quality of crops and livestock while reducing the need for intensive labor. Additionally, they focus on conserving resources like soil and water, aiming to make agriculture safer, more efficient, and sustainable.

Minimum Qualification Required

Diploma or Advanced Certificate (NQF Level 6)

Salary Range

R23 866 to R70 933 per month - 2025

FAQ

Got Questions? We’ve Got You Covered.

Do you need a college degree to get a high-paying job.
Many in-demand careers (especially in tech, trades, and design) value skills, certifications, and experience over degrees. Examples: web development, UX/UI design, cybersecurity, plumbing, etc.
The most in-demand jobs are always in tech.
While tech jobs are growing, healthcare, skilled trades, education, and renewable energy are also seeing strong demand. It's industry-specific.
AI and automation are killing all jobs.
AI is transforming jobs, not eliminating all of them. It's creating demand for new roles like AI trainers, data analysts, and cybersecurity experts..
You should choose a career based only on salary.
High-paying jobs can lead to burnout if they don’t align with your strengths or interests. Long-term success comes from balancing income with passion and growth.
Freelancing isn’t a "real" job.
Freelancers are a vital part of today’s workforce. Many earn more than traditional employees and enjoy greater flexibility and autonomy.
You should "follow your dreams" no matter what.
Dreams are important, but not all of them are financially or emotionally sustainable as careers. Sometimes, separating dream from job keeps both alive.
Corporate jobs are more respectable than blue-collar or trade work.
Many tradespeople earn more than college grads — and without student debt. Skill-based jobs like electricians, welders, and HVAC techs are essential and well-paid.
There are no jobs in South Africa.
There are jobs — but there’s a mismatch between skills and market needs. Many roles go unfilled because applicants don’t have the right training, while oversaturated degrees (like generic BAs or BComs) produce more graduates than the market can absorb.
Tertiary education guarantees employment.
South Africa has a high number of unemployed graduates. A degree alone isn’t enough — employers want work experience, digital skills, problem-solving, and adaptability. In many cases, technical/vocational training offers faster, more employable outcomes.
Degrees like law, accounting, and teaching are “safe” career bets.
These fields are oversaturated in SA. Many law and accounting graduates struggle to find articles or clerkships, and teacher placements are inconsistent. “Safe” isn’t always safe — researching demand is critical.
